KEY FINDINGS

1) JV matches the pre-mirror baseline (63.7% vs 63.6% pre-mirror) — the JV knobs
   faithfully reproduce the old AI playbook. Mirror is consistent. Sanity check passed.

2) Varsity is the WORST tier for unseating a 3-node leader (79.3%), worse than the
   old AI by 16 percentage points. The EV scoring tells the gangers "the dug-in node
   is -EV, don't bother" — so they stop attacking it. The "smarter" AI is making the
   leader STICKIER, not less.

3) Bloodbath barely improves on Varsity (77.0%) — the emergency coalition forces
   some attacks at 0.4 odds and salvages ~2 percentage points, but the underlying
   EV-pessimism dominates. Phase 4b is partially offsetting Phase 3, not solving it.

4) Sandbox (37.3%) shows the floor works — without lookahead/coordination the leader
   is genuinely beatable. Quality of the ladder shape: Sandbox < JV < Bloodbath ~ Varsity.

DIAGNOSIS
The math is clear. At a defended 3-node tile (7 troops, dug-in +2):
  Today (without #1+#3): single attacker (force+2 vs entrench+2) → ~44% per exchange.
  EV scoring with 3 gangers each at ~44% concludes: -EV, skip the attack.
  Result: leader chips slower than they refill, runs out the 3-round timer.

PRESCRIPTION (the planned levers #1 + #3 from the prior analysis)
- Lever #1: scaling anti-leader surge (+1 per coalition member, capped +3) when defender
  holds 3+ nodes. Lifts 3-ganger odds from ~44% to ~67-76% per exchange.
- Lever #3: entrenchment crack — defender's +2 dug-in bonus drops to 0 when 2+ factions
  have struck the node recently. Removes the fortress effect entirely.
- Combined target (vs Varsity baseline 79.3%): drop to ~55-65%. Phase 5 step 3+ work.
