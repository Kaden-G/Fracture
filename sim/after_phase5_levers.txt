KEY FINDINGS

1. LADDER NOW MONOTONIC IN THE RIGHT DIRECTION
   Conversion now DECREASES as tier increases above JV:
     Sandbox 34.1 > JV 60.3 (mild ramp; weak AIs leak)
     JV 60.3 > Varsity 68.6 ≈ Bloodbath 63.2 (smart play CRACKS the fortress)
   Before, Varsity 79.3% was the WORST at unseating leaders — now it's
   second-best and Bloodbath is best, as the design intended.

2. BLOODBATH IS IN TARGET BAND (55-65%)
   63.2% — a 3-node lead is now winnable but no longer near-automatic.
   The dogpile actually pays off because surge+crack make each strike
   matter, not just the count of strikers.

3. VARSITY IS CLOSE BUT ABOVE TARGET (68.6%)
   Varsity has the EV brain but not the coordinate flag, so it doesn't
   take sub-0.32 sacrifice strikes that crack the fortress for the next
   attacker. That's correct: Varsity should be "the standard challenge,"
   not Bloodbath. A ~5 pt gap between them feels right.

4. ENDGAMES ARE LONGER + MORE CONTESTED
   Avg rounds up across all tiers (Bloodbath +2.4, Varsity +1.6).
   Games timeout slightly more often. The contested middle of the streak
   distribution should be filled in (need to verify directly, but the
   round-length proxy is consistent with "leader gets pushed, recovers,
   gets pushed again" instead of "instant stalemate or instant win").

5. GHOST IS STILL OVER-TUNED
   Varsity ghost still 39.3% (fair share ~22%). The levers don't help
   here — sabotage/leapfrog dominates regardless of who's the leader.
   Logged for a future balance pass; out of scope for this phase.

INTERPRETATION
The hypothesis from step 3 — "smart AI makes the leader stickier because
the gang-up changes who attacks, not the outcome" — was correct, and the
levers fix it exactly as designed. The per-exchange chip rate matters
once you can land enough strikes to trigger the surge.
